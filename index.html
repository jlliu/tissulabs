The science behind the pillow

Tissu Labs is devoted to making the world a softer, more compassionate place by engineering for human comfort and wellbeing. To start, we carefully researched why, and how, skin-to-skin contact feels so good.

IT STARTS WITH CHILDHOOD...

The period of time surrounding infancy and early childhood are linked with oxytocin, a hormone that stimulates uterine contractions and lactation.

Often dubbed "the love hormone", oxytocin plays a crucial role in social behavior like developing secure attachments between mother and child.

OXYTOCIN: "THE LOVE HORMONE"

AND CONTINUES AS A CONDITIONED REFLEX.

When we grow up with touch in our lives, we begin associating its physical sensations - warmth, the texture of skin, the rhythm of breath - with memories being of being held (and oxytocin-flooded) as a child.

This reflex explains why we feel good when we cuddle up to something warm and soft, like another person - or even a nice blanket!
